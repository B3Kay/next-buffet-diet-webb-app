# The Buffet Diet — Brand Voice & Persona

## The Voice

Think: **carnivoreaurelius meets Nathan For You meets a shreddmaxxing protocol doc.**

Raw. Unpolished on purpose. Short punchy sentences. Scientific terms dropped casually like everyone already knows them. Absurd ideas presented as the most obvious things in the world. The humor comes from total deadpan conviction about something that sounds insane but is actually... completely logical when you think about it.

## Tone Rules

1. **Short sentences. Fragments even.** Don't write essays. Write like a Twitter thread that got out of hand.
2. **State the obvious like nobody else sees it.** "Your ancestors didn't meal prep. Nobody is talking about this."
3. **Mix bro-speak with real science.** "Autophagy is literally your cells taking out the trash. This is basic biology."
4. **Deadpan absurdity (Nathan For You energy).** Present genuinely crazy things with total seriousness. "I did a 5-day water fast and then remembered I had a half marathon. I ran it. I felt fine."
5. **Never apologize for the take.** No hedging. No "this might sound crazy but..." Just say the thing.
6. **Use "protocol" and "stack" liberally.** Everything is a protocol. Everything can be stacked.
7. **Anti-mainstream-diet energy.** Meal prep is cope. 6 small meals is propaganda. The food pyramid was a psyop.
8. **The math should speak for itself.** When you drop the numbers, let them hit. Don't oversell. The numbers are already insane.
9. **Ancestral framing.** Your great-great-grandfather didn't have a meal prep container. He ate when he killed something and fasted when he didn't. You're designed for this.
10. **Slightly competitive.** Eating at a buffet is a game. You vs. the buffet's business model. And you're winning.

## Words & Phrases That Fit

- "Nobody is talking about this"
- "This is obvious"
- "The math is insane"
- "Protocol"
- "Stack" (value stack, protein stack)
- "Ancestral" / "your ancestors"
- "Arbitrage" (for the financial angle)
- "Optimization"
- "Just think about it"
- "This is not a joke"
- "Cope" (for things that don't work)
- "It's that simple"

## Words & Phrases to Avoid

- "Comprehensive" / "holistic" / "journey"
- "I'm sorry for that joke"
- "But seriously though"
- Excessive hedging or disclaimers
- Corporate marketing speak
- Overly long paragraphs
- Formal transitions between ideas

## The Founder Character

Benjamin Karlsson. Software engineer. Trains weighted calisthenics 3-4x/week and gymnastics 3x/week. Eats carnivore. Did multiple extended fasts (3 days, 5 days). Once did a 5-day fast and then ran a half marathon. Has two all-you-can-eat buffets on each side of the road next to his office. Challenged his overweight best friend to try OMAD buffet and they turned it into a competitive sport. Looked at his bank statement and realized he was spending less money, not more. Then pushed it further — every other day, every third day. Built a whole system around it.

He presents all of this as if it's the most normal, obvious thing in the world. Like YOU'RE the weird one for not doing this already.
